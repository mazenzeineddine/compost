  • Green Ingredients

How to make compost at home

  • First, you simply need a container. It can be anything from a bucket to a normal dustbin or a garden pot. Drill some holes in the container so as to let some air in easly. After that, you will need soil and pre-made compost. And as for the minerals and nutrients, just use kitchen waste like egg shells, banana peels and any other organic waste.

  • Begin to put a layer of soil and a layer of compost of 1 inch each for the base. Then, put the egg shells to provide calcium. On top of it, put banana peels for the potasium, don't forget to cut them to make the composting process easier and faster. After that, put the other organic waste that you have, you can add leafs too. Reapeat the process and finally cover it with compost then soil. Remember thet the layers must be in touch with one another. Water the mixture well and keep it in sunlight.

  • Remember watering it when it begins to dry up, that means every 10-15 days to keep it moist. It is a good idea to mix your compost on a regular basis as it helps to aerate the pile and to fasten the reaction and the add some leafs on the top. After 60-70 days your compost is ready and can be used in the garden after taking off the leafs.
